Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese like ma used to make.

Remember back as a kid, when ma used to make you spaghetti bolognese for after your sports games?

This is sure to take you back, this spaghetti bolognese is sure to make you relive dinner with ma

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes
  • Spaghetti
  • Ground beef/pork 50/50 mix
  • Bacon
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Basil
  • Parsley
  • Bay Leaf
  • Chili flakes

Steps

  1. Crush your tomatoes. We want a fine paste, there's a reason ma could slap you so hard, that's because she worked so hard crushing these tomatoes
  2. Cook bacon. Cook your bacon, we want it still soft. Keep the pan fat.
  3. Cook ground meat. Cook your ground meat in the pan fat. Add some salt, garlic and onion as well.
  4. Boil the tomato paste. We want our paste slightly rolling.
  5. Add seasoning and meat. Add garlic, onion, basil, parsley, 1 bayleaf per person, chili flakes and meat to the sauce.
  6. Simmer the sauce. Turn down the heat to a simmer, stirring frequently. Allow to cook for another 2 hours.
  7. Cook spaghetti. Add salt to a large pot, boil. Cook spaghetti for 10-12 minutes or until soft.
  8. Strain spaghetti. Strain the spaghetti of water, keep up to 1 cup of water depending on the desired thickness of sauce.
  9. Add water to sauce, and sauce to spaghetti. You can add some water to change the consistency of the sauce. Add the sauce to the spaghetti, and mix together.
  10. Enjoy! Hopefully you get a bay-leaf for goodluck! Should last in the fridge for up to 6 days.
